Admission Inquiry · Lead Tracking
Turn lost leads into enrolled students.

⚠️ The Problem

All year, parents call, walk in, ask about admission. Their phone number, child's class, when they visited — all scribbled in a notebook that inevitably gets lost. Follow-ups never happen. Hundreds of potential students go to competitors because nobody called them back.

✅ The Solution

Each inquiry flows through a 6-stage pipeline: New → Contacted → Visited → Form filled → Enrolled → Lost. Notes, reminders, automatic SMS. A single click surfaces the leads you haven't contacted yet.

Students · Master Database
One search box, every student's full history.

⚠️ The Problem

You need a student's father's phone — hunt through the admission register. You need their last year's result — a different notebook. You need their fee ledger — the accountant's book. Each student's data is scattered across 5–10 separate places.

✅ The Solution

One master profile per student: photo, guardians, address, health info, admission date, transfer history, term-by-term result timeline, fee ledger, attendance pattern, Hifz progress, behavior notes — everything. Search by name, roll, or phone and the answer is 2 seconds away.

Classes, Sections, Subjects · Structure
Define once, the rest of the system configures itself.

⚠️ The Problem

Class 6 has 4 sections. Each section has 12 subjects. Each subject has its own pass mark. Each section has its own class teacher. Rebuilding this map every year by memory is error-prone and exhausting.

✅ The Solution

Declare it once: classes (Nursery → Class 10 / Hifz / Kitab), sections (A, B, C…), subjects (Bangla, English, Math, Islamiyāt…), subject-teacher assignments. Every downstream feature — exams, attendance, marks — obeys this structure automatically.

06
📅
Academic Years · Annual Containers
Year-end rollover without losing a single record.

⚠️ The Problem

2025 ends. The new session begins in 2026. Where do 2025's attendance records, results, and fee history go? Erasing registers to reuse them is how history disappears.

✅ The Solution

Each year is a self-contained container. Switching the active year starts the new session. The old year moves to archive mode — fully browsable, permanently read-only. Pull any old transcript on demand.

  1. Create a new year: "2026" · start 1 Jan · end 31 Dec.
  2. Set Active: all new attendance, marks, and fees belong to this year.
  3. Student promotion: one click moves all of Class 6 into Class 7. Old records stay intact.
  4. Archive: prior years remain viewable but cannot be edited.

Exams & Results · Tabulation on Autopilot
No more tabulation all-nighters.

⚠️ The Problem

Five teachers deliver marksheets. The tabulator transfers them by hand. A week of late nights, Bangla Unicode glitches, grade-calculation errors. Disputes from parents over wrong positions.

✅ The Solution

Teachers enter marks in a keyboard-friendly grid. The system computes totals, percentages, grades, positions, and GPA. Bilingual printable marksheets in two clicks.

  1. Create exam: "Midterm 2026" → start/end date → subjects.
  2. Schedule: which day, which subject. Admit cards generate automatically.
  3. Marks entry: subject teachers sign in → grid view → type fast. "Lock" → principal approves.
  4. Publish: one click → visible in every student's portal, SMS notification to guardians.
🎓 GPA 5.0 by default, or switch to percentage or a custom rubric. Marksheets mirror Bangladesh's board format.
09
📎
Assignments · Homework
Homework pages never go missing again.

⚠️ The Problem

A teacher announces homework in class. Students forget or skip writing it in the diary. When a student fails the assignment, who's at fault? During remote learning, this problem multiplies by ten.

✅ The Solution

Teachers send a digital assignment — title, description, attachment, submission date. Students and parents receive portal notifications. Reminders go out as the due date approaches. Teachers grade submissions directly.

  1. Teacher: "+ New Assignment" → title, file upload, due date.
  2. Student: notification in portal → upload answer (photo / PDF) or in-app text.
  3. Teacher: submissions list → grade + feedback.
  4. Overdue: system automatically SMSes the guardian.

Online Classes · Live & Recorded
Zoom / Meet links, in one place.

⚠️ The Problem

During COVID or floods, classes move online. Every day a new Zoom link goes out on WhatsApp — some get it, some don't. Attendance is untrackable.

✅ The Solution

The teacher schedules "Class 7A — Math — 10am — Meet link". It appears in every student's portal. Joining automatically marks attendance. The recording is saved for later viewing.

Payments · Receipt Ledger
Every rupee has a signed, auditable receipt.

⚠️ The Problem

A parent says "I paid ৳3,000 last month." Twenty minutes of register-searching. It's there — or it isn't. Cash came in but the accountant forgot to issue a receipt.

Every payment — cash, bank, bKash, Nagad — on one timeline. QR-coded receipts. Every historical payment visible inside each student's portal. If the accountant skips an entry, the principal sees the gap instantly.

Expenses · Category Tracking
Know where the money actually goes.

⚠️ The Problem

Electricity bills, book purchases, repairs, food — everything lumped into one "expense book". At month-end you can't see where the overruns are. No budget discipline.

✅ The Solution

Twenty-four pre-configured expense heads — Utility, Stationery, Food, Repair, Salary, Tax… Attach a receipt to every entry. A monthly pie chart reveals exactly which category is growing.

Donations · Campaign Ledger
Every donor deserves a transparent answer.

⚠️ The Problem

Someone donates ৳10,000. Six months later they ask "Where did my money go?" If you can't answer clearly, you won't receive another donation.

✅ The Solution

Each donation campaign (masjid construction, Eid gifts, scholarship fund) is a separate ledger. Donor name, phone, amount, and how the money was used — all tracked. Donors receive automatic quarterly reports.

Investments · Institutional Assets
Watch over the institution's long-term holdings.

⚠️ The Problem

The institution owns land, rents out shops, holds FDRs. Lease dates, rent renewals, monthly returns — no human memory holds all of it accurately.

✅ The Solution

Each asset (land, shop, FDR) gets its own record. Lease-renewal alerts fire 30 days in advance. Monthly returns auto-flow into this month's income.

17

Payroll · Salary
45 teachers' salaries — 5 minutes.

⚠️ The Problem

Month-end: basic, allowances, overtime, tax, absent-deductions, advances — each teacher is a different calculation. Two days for the accountant, and any mistake makes a teacher unhappy.

✅ The Solution

Set the pay scale once. On the 1st of every month the system auto-computes salary, allowances, and deductions. Export a bank file. Principal approves, transfers go out via bKash or bank.

18
🏆
Scholarships · Merit & Need
Rewarding brilliance, sustaining opportunity.

⚠️ The Problem

One student gets a 50% waiver, another 100%, a third a special-case grant. Applying these discounts correctly on every invoice is error-prone. Mistakes trigger parent complaints.

✅ The Solution

Each scholarship — merit-based or need-based — is defined once and linked to a student. Fee invoices apply the discount automatically. Transparent, auditable reporting.

Part 06 · 3 features

Madrasa-Specific Modules

Dedicated to religious education — Hifz, Kitab, Sabaq. Bangladesh's first and only comprehensive Islamic-education tracking system.

3 features
26
📖
Hifz Tracking · Qur'an Memorization
30 juz — every student's digital diary.

⚠️ The Problem

The ustadh writes daily: "Student X — Juz 5, page 12, line 8." Six months later, the diary is full. Old progress becomes hard to retrieve. Parents asking for an update get vague answers.

✅ The Solution

A visual timeline of each student's Hifz journey — surah, juz, pages, start date, end date. Sabaq / Sabqi / Manzil tracked separately. Parents see live progress from the portal.

27
📕
Kitab Progress · Dars-e-Nizami
Eight-year journey, stage by stage.

⚠️ The Problem

Dars-e-Nizami spans 8 years. Each year has specific kitab. Which ones has a student completed? Which are pending? Long, error-prone registers.

✅ The Solution

Pre-configured Dars-e-Nizami curriculum — Mishkat, Bukhari, Muslim, Abu Dawud, Mufkin… Each kitab shows completion %, and the exact date of completion.

28
☀️
Daily Sabaq · Lesson Tracking
Today's lesson, tomorrow's revision.

⚠️ The Problem

The madrasa concept of "sabaq" is unique: new content every day, "sabqi" revision the next day, "manzil" weekly revision. Without tracking this cycle, students fall behind quietly.

✅ The Solution

Daily sabaq entry — lesson, quality (excellent / good / weak), ustadh's remarks. A 7-day chart shows the trend. Weak scores trigger guardian alerts.

AI Risk Analysis · Dropout Prevention
AI tells you who's about to drop out — early.

⚠️ The Problem

Every year 5–10% of students disappear mid-session. Parents rarely say anything. You find out when it's too late to do anything.

✅ The Solution

AI analyzes each student's pattern (attendance ↓, marks ↓, late fees, missing assignments, paused Hifz) and assigns a "risk score". High-risk students are automatically flagged — counselors reach out proactively.

🧠 Data-driven: Not just attendance — marks trends, sabaq quality, guardian payment patterns, assignment submission rates — every signal combined.

✅ The Solution
